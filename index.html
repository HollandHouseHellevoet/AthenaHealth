Every practice on Athena's platform exports cash every month, through insurance structures designed for institutions, not independents.

The Problem

Where the Money Goes Today

Malpractice. Workers' compensation. Property. Cyber. And ten more lines just like them. Each one purchased alone, at solo-practice pricing, in a market architected for institutions.

Roughly 83 cents of every premium dollar pays claims. The remaining 17 cents does not come back. A portion covers carrier administration. Another portion funds broker commissions, paid by the carrier, not the practice, for placing the practice's own business with that carrier.

What remains becomes surplus. The carrier's retained capital. It sits on their balance sheet as equity.

Then there is float. Premiums arrive on the first of the month. Claims are paid over the following 30, 60, 90 days. In that gap the carrier holds the practice's money and invests it. Warren Buffett has described this mechanic openly for sixty years. It is why Berkshire Hathaway is also one of the largest investment portfolios in the world.

The practice on Athena's platform is funding both of those balance sheets every month. And receiving nothing in return except coverage.

That capital was not created by the carrier. It was accumulated from the premium flows of practices like those on Athena.

What MedMerge Builds

A Financial Infrastructure Platform. Built for the Independent Physician.

MedMerge sits between the independent practice and the claim, across every insurance line.

Inside the MedMerge collective, the float stays inside the structure. The surplus accumulates as equity on a balance sheet the physician owns. The capital that previously left does not leave.

Costs drop 20 to 30 percent from the first month. Not as a projection. As a function of the structure itself.

We convert expenses into assets.

The capital was already leaving. The only question is which balance sheet it builds.

The Employer Side

A Revenue Channel That Only Independence Can Access.

Large enterprise employers have spent years trying to remove the intermediary between their premium dollar and the physician's payment. The carrier. The system. The margin that disappears in between.

Direct contracting with independent physicians is what that looks like in practice. Every physician practice and physician-owned hospital inside the MedMerge network is part of that channel.

A physician who sold their practice to a health system cannot participate. The system owns the contract. An independent physician inside MedMerge can.

This is not cost savings. This is balance sheet creation.

The Mission

Built on the Same Conviction.

In 1997 Jonathan Bush and Todd Park built AthenaHealth on a specific conviction: independent medicine is worth defending, and the right infrastructure makes it possible.

From 75 percent of all physicians in 2010, the independent physician population has fallen to 15 percent today. The practices that consolidated did not leave because they wanted to. The economic architecture made staying independent untenable.

MedMerge was built on the same conviction Bush and Park started with. Applied to the layer of the problem that has not yet been solved.

Athena built the operating system for independent medicine. MedMerge builds the balance sheet.
