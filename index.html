The Architecture Nobody Fixed

Athena solved how independent practices operate. No one solved how they accumulate capital.

The Problem

Every practice on Athena's platform still exports cash every month — through insurance structures designed for institutions, not independents.

Malpractice. Workers' compensation. Property. Cyber.

Every line purchased alone. Every line priced at retail.

Premiums come in.

Claims are paid over time.

The difference becomes float.

The remainder becomes surplus.

Both stay with the carrier.

The practice funds it. The practice never owns it.

UnitedHealth holds $25.5 billion of that surplus. Elevance holds $40.3 billion.

That capital was not created by the carrier. It was accumulated from the premium flows of practices like those on Athena.

What MedMerge Builds

MedMerge keeps the float and surplus inside a physician-owned structure.

Across every insurance line, the dollars no longer leave. The equity no longer leaves.

Costs drop 20–30% immediately — not as a projection, but as a function of the structure.

We convert an expense into an asset.

Athena's Network

Athena's client base represents one of the largest independent physician networks in the country.

Today, that network exports capital every month.

MedMerge turns that same flow into retained equity — inside the ecosystem Athena already powers.

This is not cost savings. This is balance sheet creation.
